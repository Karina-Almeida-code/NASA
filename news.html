NASA issues statement of preparation for what will happen on 03/03

NASA has released guidance on the total lunar eclipse expected in the early hours of March 3. The phenomenon is expected to last approximately 58 minutes at its maximum phase and coincides with the Full Moon of the month. The event became popularly known as “Blood Moon of the Worm”, a combination that unites scientific explanation and cultural tradition.

Delay in Artemis II Mission

🚀 Highlight: Delay in Artemis II Mission Rocket Issue (02/21/2026): NASA encountered a helium flow problem in the upper stage of the SLS (Space Launch System) rocket. This interrupte d preparations for the crewed launch. March Launch Canceled: The mission chief confirmed that the launch, previously scheduled for early March 2026, will not take place in March due to this technical issue. New Schedule: The mission is expected to be postponed to late March or April 2026. The Goal: Artemis II will carry four astronauts (Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen) around the Moon on a 10-day journey , marking the first crewed lunar flight in over 50 years.
